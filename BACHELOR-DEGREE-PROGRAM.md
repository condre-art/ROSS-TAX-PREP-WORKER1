# BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN TAX ACCOUNTING
## 4-Year Degree Program - Ross Tax Academy

---

## DEGREE PROGRAM OVERVIEW

**Degree Awarded**: Bachelor of Science in Tax Accounting (B.S. Tax Accounting)  
**Total Credits Required**: 120 credit hours  
**Program Duration**: 4 years (8 semesters) full-time  
**Delivery Method**: 100% Online  
**Accreditation Status**: Pending regional accreditation  

**Program Mission**: To prepare students for professional careers in tax preparation, tax consulting, and financial services through rigorous academic study combining accounting principles, federal and state tax law, business fundamentals, and IRS compliance standards.

**Career Pathways**:
- Tax Preparer / Tax Consultant
- Enrolled Agent (after passing EA exam)
- IRS Revenue Agent (with additional federal hiring requirements)
- Corporate Tax Analyst
- Tax Software Consultant
- Bookkeeping & Accounting Services
- Financial Planning & Advisory

## PROGRAM LEARNING OUTCOMES

Upon completion, graduates will be able to:

1. **Tax Compliance**: Prepare individual and business tax returns in compliance with IRS regulations and Circular 230 standards
2. **Tax Law Application**: Apply federal tax law (Internal Revenue Code, Treasury Regulations, case law) to complex tax scenarios
3. **Financial Reporting**: Prepare financial statements using GAAP principles and analyze financial data for tax planning
4. **Audit Procedures**: Understand IRS examination procedures and represent clients in correspondence audits
5. **Ethics & Professional Responsibility**: Practice within Circular 230 ethical guidelines and maintain taxpayer confidentiality
6. **Technology Proficiency**: Use professional tax software (Drake, Intuit ProSeries, Thomson Reuters) and accounting systems (QuickBooks, Xero)
7. **Critical Thinking**: Analyze tax positions, evaluate risk, and develop tax-efficient strategies for clients
8. **Communication**: Communicate complex tax concepts clearly to clients, IRS agents, and stakeholders

## COURSE DESCRIPTIONS

### **YEAR 1 COURSES**

#### **ACCT-1301: Principles of Financial Accounting (3 Credits)**
Introduction to financial accounting including the accounting cycle, financial statements, and basic business transactions. Topics include GAAP, double-entry bookkeeping, and financial reporting.

**Prerequisites**: None  
**Textbook**: Financial Accounting (Libby, Libby, Hodge)

---

#### **ACCT-1302: Principles of Managerial Accounting (3 Credits)**
Cost behavior, cost-volume-profit analysis, budgeting, and performance evaluation. Focus on internal decision-making and management control systems.

**Prerequisites**: ACCT-1301  
**Textbook**: Managerial Accounting (Garrison, Noreen, Brewer)

---

### **YEAR 2 COURSES**

#### **TAX-2301: Individual Income Tax I (3 Credits)**
Federal income taxation of individuals including gross income, exclusions, deductions, credits, and filing status. Introduction to Form 1040 preparation.

**Prerequisites**: ACCT-1301  
**Textbook**: Federal Taxation: Comprehensive Topics (Pope, Rupert, Anderson)

---

#### **TAX-2302: Individual Income Tax II (3 Credits)**
Advanced individual taxation including capital gains/losses, passive activities, self-employment tax, AMT, and retirement planning.

**Prerequisites**: TAX-2301  
**Textbook**: Federal Taxation: Comprehensive Topics (Pope, Rupert, Anderson)

---

#### **ACCT-2301: Intermediate Financial Accounting I (3 Credits)**
In-depth study of financial accounting theory and practice. Topics include cash, receivables, inventory, PPE, and financial statement analysis.

**Prerequisites**: ACCT-1302  
**Textbook**: Intermediate Accounting (Kieso, Weygandt, Warfield)

---

#### **ACCT-2302: Intermediate Financial Accounting II (3 Credits)**
Continuation of ACCT-2301. Topics include investments, long-term liabilities, stockholders' equity, revenue recognition, and leases.

**Prerequisites**: ACCT-2301  
**Textbook**: Intermediate Accounting (Kieso, Weygandt, Warfield)

---

### **YEAR 3 COURSES**

#### **TAX-3301: Business Taxation: Corporations (3 Credits)**
Federal taxation of C corporations including formation, operations, distributions, and liquidations. Form 1120 preparation and compliance.

**Prerequisites**: TAX-2302  
**Textbook**: South-Western Federal Taxation: Corporations, Partnerships, Estates & Trusts

---

#### **TAX-3302: Business Taxation: Partnerships & S-Corps (3 Credits)**
Taxation of partnerships and S corporations including formation, operations, distributions, and special allocations. Forms 1065 and 1120-S preparation.

**Prerequisites**: TAX-3301  
**Textbook**: South-Western Federal Taxation: Corporations, Partnerships, Estates & Trusts

---

#### **TAX-3303: Tax Research & Planning (3 Credits)**
Tax research methodology using primary and secondary sources. Introduction to tax planning strategies and professional tax software.

**Prerequisites**: TAX-2302  
**Textbook**: Federal Tax Research (Raabe, Whittenburg, Sanders, Gill)

---

#### **TAX-3304: Estate & Gift Taxation (3 Credits)**
Federal taxation of estates and trusts including estate planning, gift tax, generation-skipping transfer tax, and fiduciary income tax. Form 706 and Form 1041 preparation.

**Prerequisites**: TAX-2302  
**Textbook**: Federal Estate & Gift Taxation (Stephens, Maxfield, Lind, Calfee)

---

#### **ACCT-3305: Auditing Principles (3 Credits)**
Audit planning, risk assessment, evidence gathering, and audit reporting. Professional standards (GAAS, PCAOB) and ethics.

**Prerequisites**: ACCT-2302  
**Textbook**: Auditing & Assurance Services (Arens, Elder, Beasley)

---

### **YEAR 4 COURSES**

#### **TAX-4301: Advanced Individual Tax Topics (3 Credits)**
Complex individual tax issues including like-kind exchanges, installment sales, NOL carryforwards, multi-state taxation, and tax credits.

**Prerequisites**: TAX-2302  
**Textbook**: Advanced Individual Taxation (custom materials)

---

#### **TAX-4303: IRS Procedures & Practice (3 Credits)**
IRS examination procedures, audit representation, appeals process, penalties, interest, and Circular 230 professional responsibility. Prepares students for IRS practice.

**Prerequisites**: TAX-3303  
**Textbook**: IRS Practice & Procedure (Saltzman & Book)

---

#### **TAX-4305: State & Local Taxation (3 Credits)**
State income tax, sales and use tax, property tax, and local business taxes. Multi-state compliance and nexus issues.

**Prerequisites**: TAX-3301  
**Textbook**: State & Local Taxation (Hellerstein, Hellerstein, Swain)

---

#### **TAX-4302: Tax Practice Management (3 Credits)**
Operating a tax practice including client management, fee structures, liability insurance, engagement letters, quality control, and practice development.

**Prerequisites**: TAX-4303  
**Textbook**: Managing Your Tax Season (custom materials)

---

#### **TAX-4304: International Taxation (3 Credits)**
U.S. taxation of foreign income, foreign tax credit, transfer pricing, and tax treaties. Introduction to global tax planning.

**Prerequisites**: TAX-3301  
**Textbook**: International Taxation (Kuntz & Peroni)

---

#### **TAX-4401: Tax Capstone Project (3 Credits)**
Comprehensive tax case study requiring students to prepare multiple tax returns, conduct tax research, and present findings. Simulates real-world tax consulting engagement.

**Prerequisites**: Senior standing, completion of all tax major courses  
**Format**: Project-based, team collaboration

Online courses require weekly participation in discussion boards, completion of assignments, and attendance at live weekly lectures (recorded for asynchronous viewing).

Plagiarism, cheating, and unauthorized collaboration are strictly prohibited and may result in course failure or dismissal from the program.

Midterm and final exams require proctoring via webcam monitoring or approved testing center.

Students may withdraw from a course with a "W" grade before the withdrawal deadline (typically Week 12 of 16-week semester). Refunds follow standard institutional policy.
