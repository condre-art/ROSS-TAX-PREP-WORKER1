POLICY 100: Governance & Institutional Authority

Policy Statement

Ross Tax Academy maintains defined governance structures that separate academic, administrative, and compliance functions. Final academic authority rests with institutional leadership, ensuring consistent application of policies and standards.

Governance Framework

The institution establishes clear roles and responsibilities for:

  • Executive Leadership: Director of Institutional Operations (final authority)
  • Academic Leadership: Curriculum committee and instructor oversight
  • Administrative Leadership: Student services, enrollment, and compliance
  • Compliance Officer: Policy enforcement and regulatory alignment

Decision-Making Authority

All academic and disciplinary decisions are made by authorized personnel and documented in institutional records. No instructor, administrator, or staff member may bypass established procedures or policies.

POLICY 200: Academic Integrity

Policy Statement

Academic integrity is foundational to the institution's mission. All instruction, assessments, and certifications are governed by integrity standards. Violations are subject to review and disciplinary action.

Integrity Standards

  • Honesty: All submitted work must be the student's own or properly attributed
  • Authenticity: Assessments must reflect the student's actual knowledge and skills
  • Fairness: Students must not gain unfair advantage through deception or system abuse
  • Accountability: Students are responsible for understanding and upholding integrity standards

POLICY 210: Zero-Tolerance Code of Conduct

Policy Statement (Zero-Tolerance)

The institution maintains a zero-tolerance policy for conduct violations. Disrespect, dishonesty, theft, cheating, system abuse, or repeated non-compliance results in immediate disciplinary action, including dismissal.

Prohibited Conduct

  • Academic Dishonesty: Cheating, plagiarism, contract cheating, unauthorized assistance
  • Disrespect: Harassment, threats, or abusive language toward instructors, staff, or peers
  • Fraud: Misrepresentation in applications, records, or assessments
  • Theft: Unauthorized access to materials, data, or systems
  • System Abuse: Circumventing LMS controls, test security, or access restrictions
  • Repeated Non-Compliance: Five (5) unexcused absences constitute automatic violation

Enforcement

No Discretion: Instructors have no authority to excuse or negotiate zero-tolerance violations. All violations are reported immediately to the Compliance Officer for enforcement.

Consequences

  • Immediate Dismissal: Student is removed from program without refund
  • Certificate Revocation: Any previously issued certificates are revoked
  • Permanent Record: Violation is recorded in institutional files and disclosed to prospective employers upon request (with student consent)

POLICY 300: Curriculum Approval & Instructional Control

Policy Statement

All curriculum is institution-approved. Instructors deliver approved content only. AI tools support learning but do not replace academic judgment or instructional authority.

Curriculum Development

  • Instructor-Developed: Instructors design lessons aligned to stated learning outcomes
  • Institution-Approved: All curriculum is reviewed and approved before delivery
  • Standards-Aligned: Curriculum addresses competency requirements and workforce readiness
  • Periodic Review: Curriculum is reviewed annually and updated as needed

Instructional Control (Critical)

  • AI Tools Support Learning Only: AI tools may assist with explanations, practice problems, and study guides
  • AI Does Not Grade: AI tools cannot assign final grades or determine passing/failing status
  • Manual Oversight: All certificates and completion determinations are manually verified by instructors
  • Instructor Authority: Final academic decisions rest with qualified instructors, not systems or algorithms

POLICY 310: Required RTB Textbook

Policy Statement

All academic courses offered by the institution require the purchase and use of the RTB Textbook as a core instructional resource. Enrollment in any course constitutes acknowledgment of this requirement.

Textbook Requirement

The RTB Textbook is designated as the primary instructional text required for:

  • Course participation and lesson comprehension
  • Assessments and quizzes (content-aligned)
  • AI-assisted study tools (content reference only)

Proof of Access: Students must obtain and confirm access to the RTB Textbook prior to or at course start. Access confirmation may be required within the LMS before course progression is unlocked.

Textbook Access & Purchase

The RTB Textbook may be purchased through:

  • Direct institutional purchase (included in tuition)
  • Approved vendor or distribution partner
  • Physical or eBook (approved equivalent)

Refund & Withdrawal Notice (Non-Refundable)

Textbook purchases are non-refundable unless otherwise stated by the vendor. Withdrawal from a course does not waive the textbook requirement or permit a refund.

POLICY 311: RTB Textbook eBook Availability

Policy Statement

The RTB Textbook is available in electronic book (eBook) format and is approved as an equivalent alternative to the physical textbook for all academic courses. Students may satisfy the required course materials obligation through authorized eBook access.

eBook Access Methods

The RTB eBook may be provided through:

  • Secure LMS-integrated reader
  • Protected PDF (DRM or access-controlled)
  • Authorized third-party eBook platform
  • Institution-managed digital library access

Access Controls (Compliance-Safe)

  • Individual student access credentials (non-transferable)
  • Download, print, or copy restrictions where applicable
  • Access duration aligned with enrollment period
  • Audit logging of access (recommended)

Academic Equivalency

The RTB eBook contains the same instructional content as the printed edition and is approved for:

  • Instruction and lesson delivery
  • Assessments and quiz alignment
  • AI-assisted study support

No Academic Disadvantage: There is no academic disadvantage between physical and electronic formats.

POLICY 400: Faculty Qualifications & Oversight

Policy Statement

All instructors meet defined qualification standards and execute instructional agreements and confidentiality obligations. Instructor oversight and activity logging is enabled for compliance and quality assurance.

Instructor Qualifications

  • Education: Minimum Bachelor's degree in related field or equivalent professional credential
  • Experience: Minimum 3 years professional experience in tax, accounting, or business practice
  • Competency: Demonstrated expertise in course subject matter
  • Compliance: Agreement to institutional policies and procedures

Instructor Agreements

All instructors execute:

  • Instructional Services Agreement: Defining scope of services and institutional authority
  • Confidentiality Agreement (NDA): Protecting student data, course materials, and institutional processes
  • Code of Conduct Agreement: Commitment to ethical and professional conduct

Activity Logging & Monitoring

  • All instructor actions (grading, messaging, content updates) are logged immutably
  • Activity logs are auditable for compliance and quality review
  • Performance metrics tracked for continuous improvement

POLICY 500: Student Records & Privacy

Policy Statement

Student records are maintained securely with access controls, audit logs, and retention policies. Personally identifiable information (PII) is encrypted and protected in accordance with applicable privacy laws.

Records Maintained

  • Enrollment status and course progress (timestamped)
  • Academic performance and grades
  • Certificate issuance and revocation history
  • Financial account and payment status
  • Disciplinary actions and conduct records

Data Security

  • Encryption: PII encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS)
  • Access Control: Role-based access (RBAC) restricts viewing to authorized personnel
  • Audit Logging: All record access logged and immutable
  • Retention: Records retained per applicable regulatory requirements (minimum 7 years)

Student Rights

Students may:

  • Request copies of their records in writing
  • Challenge accuracy of information in their file
  • Request amendment of incorrect records

POLICY 600: Assessment & Completion Standards

Policy Statement

Course completion requires documented participation, assessment, and verification. All assessments are tied to stated learning outcomes.

Assessment Methods

  • Quizzes: Formative assessment aligned to lesson objectives
  • Exams: Summative assessment verifying course competency
  • Projects: Application-based assessment demonstrating skill transfer
  • Participation: Engagement metrics (attendance, discussion, timeliness)

Passing Criteria

  • Minimum Score: 70% or higher on final assessments (unless otherwise stated)
  • Completion: 100% of required assignments and modules
  • Attendance: Minimum engagement (fewer than 5 unexcused absences)

Retakes & Remediation

  • Quiz Retakes: Limited to 3 attempts per quiz (lowest score dropped if applicable)
  • Exam Retakes: One (1) retake permitted if initial score below passing threshold
  • Remediation: Failed assessments may require instructor-prescribed study before retake

POLICY 700: Credential Issuance & Revocation

Policy Statement

Certificates are issued upon verified completion and may be revoked for cause. All certificates include unique verification codes and are subject to public verification.

Certificate Eligibility

Certificates are issued when student:

  • Completes all required courses and modules
  • Achieves passing grades on all assessments
  • Satisfies attendance/participation requirements
  • Clears any outstanding account balances

Certificate Components

  • Unique verification code (RTA-{timestamp}-{6-digit code})
  • QR code for public verification without login
  • Student name, program title, issue date
  • Instructor signature and institutional seal

Certificate Revocation

Certificates may be revoked for:

  • Discovered academic dishonesty or fraud
  • Misrepresentation in enrollment or credentials
  • Violation of conduct policies (zero-tolerance)
  • Identity verification failure post-issuance

Revocation Process

  • Documented in writing to student
  • Recorded in institutional files (permanent)
  • Marked as invalid in public verification system
  • No appeal available (zero-tolerance conduct)

POLICY 800: Technology, Security & LMS Controls

Policy Statement

The institution implements role-based access control (RBAC), encryption, and continuous monitoring. All systems maintain audit readiness.

Security Controls

  • Role-Based Access (RBAC): Five roles (Super Admin, Admin, Instructor, Auditor, Student) with granular permissions
  • Encryption: AES-256 at rest, TLS in transit
  • Authentication: Secure login with optional MFA for staff
  • Audit Logs: Immutable activity logs of all system actions

Distance Education Requirements

  • Regular & Substantive Interaction (RSI): Instructors provide predictable, direct engagement tied to teaching/learning/assessment
  • Identity Verification: Student verified as same person throughout course (secure login + optional proctoring)
  • Progress Monitoring: LMS tracks completion, time-on-task, and engagement

Data Backup & Disaster Recovery

  • Daily encrypted backups of all student records
  • Disaster recovery procedures tested annually
  • Service Level Agreement (SLA): 99.5% uptime commitment

POLICY 900: Financial Compliance & Aid Disclosure

Policy Statement

The institution clearly discloses financial obligations, payment terms, and limitations regarding federal financial aid. No direct FAFSA claims are made unless accredited.

Financial Aid Disclosure (Critical)

IMPORTANT: This institution does not currently participate in federal Title IV student financial aid programs. Availability of financial aid is limited to:

  • Employer Reimbursement: Direct employer sponsorship
  • Partner Programs: Accredited institutions with whom we partner
  • Workforce Grants: State/federal workforce development (WIOA, etc.)

No guarantee of financial aid eligibility is expressed or implied.

FAFSA & Partner-Based Language (Compliant)

The institution does not represent federal financial aid eligibility unless programs are delivered through accredited partner institutions. When delivered through partners, students access FAFSA and financial aid through the partner, not the institution.

Cost Transparency

  • All costs itemized in enrollment agreement (tuition, fees, textbooks)
  • Cost estimates provided in good faith for variable costs (e.g., extended payment plans)
  • Refund policy clearly disclosed (14-day window, non-refundable items)
