3. PAYMENT & REFUND POLICY

3.1 Payment Methods

The institution accepts payment through the following methods:

  • Full Payment: Pay entire tuition upfront at enrollment
  • Payment Plans: 4, 6, 8, or 12 equal monthly installments at 0% APR
  • Employer Reimbursement: Institution coordinates with employer billing departments
  • Financial Aid (Partner Programs): When enrolled through accredited partner institutions

3.2 Tuition Refund Policy

Refund Window: Refunds are available only within 14 calendar days of enrollment and initial payment.

Refund Eligibility:

  • Student must request cancellation in writing within 14 days of enrollment
  • Tuition (instruction) is refundable during the refund window
  • RTB Textbook purchases are non-refundable once access is granted
  • Technology and administrative fees are non-refundable once services commence

3.3 Withdrawal & Tuition Liability

After 14-Day Refund Window: Students remain liable for full tuition regardless of withdrawal date or reason. The institution may accommodate hardship requests on a case-by-case basis through formal appeal (see Section 8).

Certificate Programs: Withdrawal from a certificate program does not waive the required textbook obligation or refund the textbook purchase.

3.4 Payment Plan Defaults

Students on payment plans who miss two (2) consecutive payments will have their account suspended pending payment or formal withdrawal. Late payments may result in course access restriction.

4. REQUIRED TEXTBOOK POLICY (RTB TEXTBOOK)

4.1 Textbook Requirement

All courses offered by the institution require the purchase and use of the RTB Textbook (Ross Tax Business professional education reference text) as the primary instructional resource.

4.2 Textbook Access Options

Students may satisfy the required textbook obligation through:

  • Physical Copy: Printed edition (recommended for note-taking)
  • eBook: Electronic version with DRM protection and access-control (approved equivalent)
  • Bundled: Included in tuition as a line-item fee

4.3 eBook Access & Delivery

When selecting the eBook option, students receive:

  • Secure digital access via LMS reader or protected PDF
  • Individual access credentials (non-transferable)
  • Access duration aligned with enrollment period
  • Download and print restrictions per vendor terms
  • Audit logging of access (recommended for compliance)

4.4 Textbook Refund & Withdrawal Notice

Textbook Purchases Are Non-Refundable
Once textbook access is granted (physical delivery or eBook unlock), the purchase is non-refundable regardless of course withdrawal or program completion status. This policy aligns with common higher education practice for consumable and access-based materials.

4.5 AI & Study Support Disclosure

AI-assisted learning tools within the LMS may reference the RTB Textbook for explanations, summaries, and practice support. However, AI tools do not replace:

  • Required textbook reading
  • Instructor guidance and feedback
  • Academic judgment in assessments

5. ACADEMIC POLICIES & REQUIREMENTS

5.1 Course Participation & Attendance

Distance Education Engagement: Students are expected to participate actively in online coursework, including:

  • Completing assigned lessons and modules on schedule
  • Submitting assessments and assignments by deadlines
  • Engaging in instructor discussions and feedback loops
  • Maintaining regular LMS login activity

Unexcused Absences: Five (5) consecutive weeks without course activity or submission may result in automatic course withdrawal and loss of enrollment.

5.2 Learning Outcomes & Assessment

All courses include documented learning objectives aligned to assessments. Students must:

  • Achieve passing scores on quizzes and exams (typically 70% or higher)
  • Complete all required assignments and projects
  • Demonstrate competency tied to stated learning outcomes

5.3 Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)

Pace: Students must complete at least 50% of program credits/clock hours per term.
Grade Standard: Maintain minimum grade of C (2.0 GPA) or equivalent passing score.
Maximum Timeframe: Complete program within 150% of published program length (e.g., 12 months for an 8-week certificate).
Probation & Dismissal: Failure to meet SAP may result in academic probation or dismissal.

5.4 Academic Integrity & Zero-Tolerance Conduct

Zero-Tolerance Policy:
The institution maintains a zero-tolerance code of conduct for:

  • Cheating, plagiarism, or academic dishonesty
  • Disrespect toward instructors, staff, or peers
  • Dishonesty or fraud in applications or records
  • Theft or unauthorized access to systems
  • Violation of academic integrity policies

Consequences: Violations may result in immediate dismissal from the program without refund, or revocation of previously issued certificates.

5.5 Identity Verification (Distance Education)

To ensure academic integrity in distance education, the institution verifies that the enrolled student is the same student participating and completing coursework through:

  • Secure login credentials (username/password)
  • Proctored assessments with identity verification (high-stakes exams)
  • LMS activity logging and timestamp verification

6. CERTIFICATE ISSUANCE & REVOCATION

6.1 Certificate Eligibility

Certificates are issued upon verified completion of all program requirements, including:

  • Completion of all required courses and modules
  • Passing grades on all assessments
  • Satisfaction of attendance/participation requirements
  • Clearance of any outstanding account balances or obligations

6.2 Certificate Verification & Credentials

All certificates include:

  • Unique verification code (RTA-{timestamp}-{6-digit random code})
  • QR code for public verification without login
  • Issue date, student name, and program title
  • Instructor signature and institutional seal

Public Verification: Third parties (employers, agencies) may verify certificates via QR code at no cost through the institution's public verification portal.

6.3 Certificate Revocation

The institution reserves the right to revoke certificates for cause, including:

  • Discovered academic dishonesty or fraud in assessment
  • Misrepresentation in enrollment or credentials
  • Violation of conduct policies
  • Identity verification failure post-issuance

Process: Revocation is documented in writing to the student and recorded in institutional records. Revoked certificates are marked as invalid in the public verification system.

8. APPEALS & GRIEVANCE PROCESS

8.1 Grounds for Appeal

Appeals are permitted only for alleged procedural errors in enforcement of institutional policies. Appeals may NOT challenge:

  • Institutional academic standards or policies
  • Factual findings related to misconduct
  • Grade determinations or assessment outcomes
  • Personal hardship or requests for leniency

8.2 Permitted Appeal Grounds

  • Documented failure to follow published procedures
  • Clerical or administrative error
  • Identity verification error

8.3 Appeal Process

Step 1: Student submits written appeal within 14 days of receiving adverse decision.
Step 2: Compliance officer reviews appeal and supporting documentation.
Step 3: Institution provides written decision within 10 business days.
Step 4: All appeal decisions are final; no further internal review available.

Important: Filing an appeal does not stay enforcement actions (e.g., course suspension, dismissal).
