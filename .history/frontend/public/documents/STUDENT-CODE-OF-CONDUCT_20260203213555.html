STUDENT CODE OF CONDUCT (Plain Language Guide)

Ross Tax Academy | Plain Language Version
This guide explains what we expect from you in clear, straightforward language.

What Is the Code of Conduct?

The Code of Conduct is a set of behavioral expectations that all students must follow. These rules help us maintain a respectful, honest, and secure learning environment for everyone.

Important: Violating the Code of Conduct can result in immediate removal from the program without refund, loss of your certificate, or both.

What You MUST Do

✓Be Respectful

  • Treat instructors, staff, and classmates with courtesy
  • Use professional language in all communications (email, discussion forums, messages)
  • Accept feedback from instructors without hostility or argument
  • Respect the views and opinions of others, even when you disagree

✓Be Honest

  • Complete all assignments yourself (unless group work is assigned)
  • Do not copy or plagiarize work from others or the internet
  • Accurately represent your identity and qualifications in applications
  • Take all exams and quizzes without outside help or resources
  • Report errors in your grades or records honestly

✓Attend and Participate

  • Log into the course regularly (at least weekly)
  • Submit assignments by the published deadlines
  • Engage with course materials and participate in discussions
  • If you need an excused absence, notify your instructor in advance when possible
  • Important: Five (5) unexcused absences may result in automatic removal from the course

✓Follow Policies

  • Read and understand all institutional policies and procedures
  • Follow LMS rules and access requirements
  • Do not attempt to bypass or hack into systems
  • Do not share login credentials with anyone
  • Use technology for educational purposes only

What You MUST NOT Do

✗Do NOT Cheat

  • Do not copy work from classmates or the internet
  • Do not use unauthorized resources during exams or quizzes
  • Do not have someone else complete your assignments
  • Do not share your login credentials with others
  • Do not submit the same work in multiple courses without permission

✗Do NOT Be Disrespectful

  • Do not use profanity or harsh language
  • Do not threaten, harass, or intimidate instructors or classmates
  • Do not post inflammatory or demeaning comments
  • Do not argue with instructors about their professional judgment
  • Do not blame others for your poor performance or missed deadlines

✗Do NOT Be Dishonest

  • Do not lie about your attendance, qualifications, or circumstances
  • Do not provide false information in enrollment forms
  • Do not misrepresent your identity or credentials
  • Do not claim someone else's work as your own

✗Do NOT Steal or Abuse Systems

  • Do not attempt to access materials you are not authorized to view
  • Do not download or share course materials without permission
  • Do not attempt to bypass LMS security or access controls
  • Do not use institutional resources for personal profit or commercial purposes

✗Do NOT Abandon the Course

  • Do not disappear from the course without communication
  • Five (5) unexcused absences = automatic removal
  • If you need to withdraw, notify your instructor in writing
  • Do not assume inactivity is the same as official withdrawal

What Happens If You Violate the Code?

Zero-Tolerance Violations

Immediate Removal (No Refund)

  • You are immediately removed from the program
  • You lose access to all course materials and your LMS account
  • No refund of tuition, fees, or textbook purchases
  • You are ineligible to re-enroll in any future programs

Certificate Revocation

  • Any certificate you have already earned is cancelled
  • The certificate is removed from our public verification system
  • You cannot use the certificate for employment or other purposes
  • The violation is permanently recorded in your file

Permanent Record

  • The violation is permanently recorded in institutional files
  • If an employer or regulator requests information about your enrollment, we may disclose the violation (with your consent)
  • You cannot dispute or appeal the decision

Attendance & Unexcused Absences

What Counts as an Absence?

An absence occurs when you do not log into your course for one (1) full week and do not submit any assignments or participate in any activities.

Excused vs. Unexcused Absences

Excused: You contact your instructor before you will miss a week and explain your situation (illness, family emergency, etc.). Your instructor may grant an excused absence.

Unexcused: You disappear without communicating with your instructor. After five (5) unexcused absences, you are automatically removed from the course.

Example Timeline

  • Week 1 absence (unexcused): Instructor sends email reminder
  • Week 2 absence (unexcused): Compliance notification sent
  • Week 3, 4, 5 absences (unexcused): Additional warnings
  • Week 5 absence (unexcused): You are removed from the program (final, no appeal)

Can You Appeal a Violation?

Important: No Appeals for Misconduct

If you violate the Code of Conduct, you cannot appeal the decision. There is no recourse or second chance. The decision is final.

What CAN You Appeal?

You may submit an appeal ONLY if you believe the institution made a procedural error, such as:

  • Wrong person was penalized (identity error)
  • The institution failed to follow its published procedures
  • A clerical mistake occurred in your record

What Can NOT You Appeal?

  • You disagree with the Code of Conduct policy itself
  • You claim hardship or personal circumstances
  • You claim you didn't know the rule
  • You ask for leniency or a second chance
  • You dispute the fact that you cheated, were disrespectful, or violated conduct rules

If you appeal: You have 14 days to submit a written appeal. The institution will respond within 10 business days. The decision is final.

Questions About the Code of Conduct?

If you don't understand a rule, ASK. Contact your instructor or the compliance office before you violate a policy. We'd rather answer questions than enforce penalties.
